Industrial Pipeline
Cleaning & Monitoring Robot

A state-of-the-art robotic solution designed to navigate, clean, and conduct real-time monitoring of pipelines.

Problem Statement

Conventional pipe cleaning methods are labor-intensive, time-consuming, and often inefficient. Blockages, sediment buildup, and undetected damages like cracks or leaks reduce efficiency across industrial and municipal applications. There is a critical need for autonomous systems that navigate, clean, and detect structural issues remotely.

Project Objectives

Mechanical

Develop a motorized rotating brush and scrubber for internal pipe cleaning.

Intelligence

ESP32-based control for wireless operation and real-time Wi-Fi camera monitoring.

Safety

Integration of ultrasonic sensors to detect structural damage or cracks.

Adaptability

A compact, modular design suitable for various pipe diameters.

Methodology

Concept Solution

The robot functions as a critical artery maintenance tool. It utilizes an ESP32 Controller, L298N Motor Driver, and Gear Motors to manage movement. The system uses a 5V power supply to drive the logic while a 12V Li-ion pack powers the motors.

Work Completed

Initial electronics assembly including the ESP32 and motor wiring has been finalized. Prototype chassis testing inside industrial-grade pipes is underway to ensure stability.
